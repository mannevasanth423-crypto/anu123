// chapter 01 — because neither of us knew anything

What actually is
Formula 1?

We both had no idea. I've done the research. Here's everything — explained like I'm texting you, not reading out Wikipedia.

🏎️
The Cars
The fastest road-circuit racing machines ever built. Zero to 100 km/h in 2.5 seconds. Top speed over 350 km/h. So aerodynamically extreme they generate more downforce than their own body weight — at full speed, they could drive upside down in a tunnel. Each car costs $15–20 million. The steering wheel alone is $65,000 and has 25 buttons on it.
"It's what happens when you give aerospace engineers a racing problem and tell them money doesn't matter."
📅
The Season
24 races. Different countries every two weeks. Each race is a Grand Prix. Points go top-10 — 25 for 1st down to 1 for 10th. The driver with the most wins the World Drivers' Championship. Teams with the most combined win Constructors'. It runs March to December and the drama never fully stops.
"The World Cup happens every 4 years. F1 happens every 2 weeks, year round, in a different country. Different frequency, different addiction."
📍
The Circuits
Some purpose-built, some on real city streets. Monaco uses the actual roads of Monte Carlo. Singapore races under 1,500 floodlights at night. Las Vegas runs down the Strip. Each circuit has a personality: Monza rewards pure speed, Monaco rewards millimetre precision, Spa rewards bravery in unpredictable weather.
"Monaco is so narrow two cars side by side is barely possible. 20 of them try to start it every year anyway."
🛞
Tyres & Pit Stops
Three compounds: Soft (fastest, wears fast), Medium (balanced), Hard (durable). Every driver must use at least two per race — meaning everyone pits. The stop: 2.5 seconds, 20 people. But the strategy — when to pit, which tyre, how many stops — is where races are actually won. Smarter timing regularly beats faster cars.
"More races won in the pit lane on a laptop than on the track. Both are happening simultaneously."
🔓
DRS — The Overtake Weapon
Within 1 second of the car ahead, in designated zones, you can open your rear wing and gain ~15 km/h straight-line. Without DRS, following closely is nearly impossible due to turbulent air. DRS zones are where you hold your breath. When it activates behind someone on a straight — that's F1's most electric moment.
"The legal overtake button. You'll know when it's happening."
🚗
Safety Car = Free Drama
A crash brings out the Safety Car — everyone queues in race order. Teams pit for free tyres during this window. A late Safety Car with 5 laps left can completely flip a result that took 80 laps to build. It happens regularly. When those boards go out, commentators lose their minds and they're right to.
"Late Safety Car = the race restarts. Everything built over 80 laps reshuffled in one lap."
🏆
The Championship
24-race marathon. In 2021, Verstappen overtook Hamilton on the very last lap of the very last race to win the title. The entire sport was in shock for days. A bad start doesn't end you. You can lead by 50 points and blow it. Nothing is settled until Abu Dhabi in December. By race 5, we'll be doing championship maths together.
"Nothing is decided until December. That's what makes 24 races feel like one long, impossible story."
⚖️
Penalties & Drama
Stewards review every incident — dangerous moves, track limit violations, contact. 5-second, 10-second time additions. Every decision is immediately debated by millions online. About 70% of all post-race social media is either "that was a penalty" or "that absolutely was not a penalty" and nobody ever agrees. It's part of the culture. You'll have opinions within two races.
"The stewards giveth and the stewards taketh. Usually from whoever you're currently rooting for."

Monaco Race Week
The harbour fills with over $350 million in superyachts during race week. People watch from the decks. Streets become a catwalk. Cannes Film Festival runs the same week, 45 minutes along the coast. Royalty, tech founders, celebrities, racing legends — everyone in one tiny principality for one extraordinary week. Walking Monte Carlo's streets during the GP is an experience that makes everything else feel small.
📍 The Monaco GP has run since 1929. The circuit fee: reportedly zero. Prestige considered sufficient payment.
🎪
The Paddock Club
F1's most exclusive hospitality. Above the pit lane — eye-level views of the cars. Open bar. Proper food. Pit lane access before the start. At Monaco: $5,000–$12,000 per day per person. Business deals happen here. Tom Cruise has been here. Hamilton walks past you to his car. It's not a VIP area. It is a completely different reality.
💡 At Silverstone, Paddock Club stretches 200 metres along the pit lane. Cars pass underneath at 300 km/h.
⭐
The Celebrity World
F1 has the highest average household income fanbase of any sport. The paddock: European racing money meets Silicon Valley meets Hollywood. Rihanna, LeBron James, Pharrell, Usher — regulars. Hamilton attends the Met Gala in Valentino. Leclerc photographed at Milan Fashion Week. Netflix's Drive to Survive made this mainstream culture. We showed up at exactly the right time.
📺 Drive to Survive on Netflix. We are watching this together. Non-negotiable. It's genuinely great TV.
🌍
A City That Moves
The entire F1 paddock — 10 teams, thousands of staff, hundreds of tonnes of equipment — relocates to a new country every two weeks. The only sport where the venue is the product. The paddock is a self-contained city worth billions. Each race builds up and then completely disappears. Watching it live is something that genuinely cannot be replicated on a screen.
✈️ Singapore GP builds and dismantles 300,000 LED lights every year. Up for 2 weeks. Then gone.
🏎️
The Physics Is Insane
Drivers experience up to 6G in corners — six times their body weight pressing on them at once. Heart rate averages 170bpm for 90 minutes straight. F1 drivers are among the most physically conditioned athletes on earth. They just do it at 300 km/h in a carbon fibre shell where the gap between 1st and 20th is measured in milliseconds across a 9-month season.
🏋️ Verstappen averages 190bpm during qualifying. For 90 straight seconds. Repeatedly. Per session.
💰
The Scale of It
Budget cap per team: $145M a year. Red Bull's total operation is estimated at $400M+. Hamilton earns ~$55M annually. A single engine lasts 3 races before complete rebuild. The sport sold for $8 billion in 2016 and grows every year. F1 is not a sport with money around it. Money, with a sport built inside. Our F1 account is the entry ticket to watching all of this happen.
💡 The Monaco trophy given to the winner is the real original. Not a replica. The actual trophy. Only race that does this.

The Rules That
Actually Matter

F1 has hundreds of regulations. Here are the seven that explain 90% of what you'll see on screen.

01
Qualifying Sets Sunday's Grid
Saturday qualifying: one flying lap each. The fastest starts first — pole position. At street circuits like Monaco, starting first is nearly decisive because overtaking is near-impossible. A yellow flag, a traffic car, one mistake on that one lap ruins an entire weekend. Saturday matters as much as Sunday.
🏁 "Set the alarm for Saturday too. Qualifying is sometimes more exciting than the race."
02
DRS — Within 1 Second = Weapon Unlocked
Within 1 second of the car ahead in designated zones, you can open your rear wing and gain ~15 km/h. Without DRS, following closely is near-impossible. DRS zones are where lead changes happen. Activating it on a straight to hunt down someone ahead is F1's most electric moment.
🔓 "You'll hear 'DRS is open' and immediately understand why it matters."
03
Two Tyre Compounds — Mandatory
Every driver in a dry race uses at least two tyre types. Everyone pits at least once. The strategy — when, which compound, how many stops — can be worth 10+ seconds. Smarter timing has beaten faster cars. Regularly. The laptop race runs alongside the car race and both decide who wins.
🛞 "More races won in the pit lane than on the track."
04
Safety Car = Chaos Engine
A crash brings out the Safety Car. Everyone queues in race order. Teams pile in for free tyres. A late Safety Car with 5 laps left can completely flip a result that took 80 laps to build. When those boards go out, every team recalculates and every commentator starts shouting. Good.
🚗 "Late Safety Car = the race restarts. 80 laps of work undone in one."
05
Track Limits — The White Lines
Drivers must keep at least part of the car within the white lines. Over them = invalidated qualifying lap or race time penalties. About 70% of post-race online argument involves track limits. Every race. Every weekend. Without exception. You will have opinions about this within two races.
⚖️ "White lines. Everyone knows. Every race, someone forgets."
06
Points — It's a Marathon
1st = 25pts, 2nd = 18, down to 1pt for 10th, plus fastest lap bonus. 24 races. A bad start doesn't end you. In 2021, Verstappen overtook Hamilton on the last lap of the last race of the season to win by 8 points. Nothing is settled until Abu Dhabi in December. Nothing.
🏆 "By May, you will be doing championship maths on a Sunday morning. I already know it."
07
Sprint Weekends — Double Drama
Six weekends a year: a short Saturday race (30% distance) with its own points, plus the full Sunday race. Two qualifying sessions, two races. Sprint weekends are exhausting and extraordinary and the paddock always complains about them and then produces incredible racing anyway.
🏃 "Sprint weekend = twice the drama. We love a sprint weekend."
